Ridership Summary

An examination of ridership compared to previous years.

Ridership is up 35% from last April.

A huge thanks to our operations and fleet management teams for making this possible!

How are we making a difference for new and returning bikers?

  • An overall fleet increase of 2,540 vehicles between this April and last April.
  • Continued initiatives to promote green commuting.
  • An overall station increase of 19 stations strategically placed around the city between this April and last April.

Though there are a number of initiatives in place for increasing ridership, we cannot solely attribute the success of the fleet to those initiatives alone. Our operations team and maintenance team is a huge part of Citi Bike's success. From rebalancing and station clean-up, to upkeep and repair, our operations and maintanence teams work around the clock to give our riders the best experience possible.

Subscriber Summary

An examination of subscriber and customer conversion compared to previous years.

The total number of Subscribed bikers increased 33% from last April.

Perhaps more telling is the increase in total Customer bikers by 53% from this time last year.

How are we converting and retaining Subscribed bikers?

  • A B testing of varying promotional rate time periods to give our trial subscribers more time to choose.
  • Keeping subscriber yearly rates competitive with New York City microtransit rental rates.
  • Increased station availability means both Subscribers and Customers can ride farther reliably.

Station Popularity

How does Citi Bike change the way New Yorkers Commute?

New York is always changing. A hard strategy for adapting to the needs of New Yorkers is key to meeting demand.

Station strategy is based on the popularity of each start and end station location.

Here are some of the ways Citi Bike has shifted station location strategy based on the popularity of each station, which is calculated by tracking the number of rides that depart from each station and arrive to each station.

  • By adding 19 new stations to the available departure and arrival roster.
  • By shifting poorly performing stations to areas in the vicinity where they will be more useful, such as shifting the W 64th St and West End Ave station west two blocks to W 64th St and Thelonious Monk Circle.

Though the charts display an increase in popularity of certain stations, it's important to remember that ridership did increase overall by 35%. The appearance of an increase in popularity of a station can be as easily attributed to the overall ridership increase as it can be attributed to a any meaningful pattern in biker use.

Peak Usage Hours

When do our bikers want to ride?

Early morning and evening are the most popular times for bikers to ride.

Even without clear visualization, it is not difficult to imagine that demand increases during New York's busy morning and evening work commute times.

Our peak usage hours give us a clear indication of where to focus our efforts when rebalancing bikes to New York's morning and evening commute points.
